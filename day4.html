One Last Breakfast, Then Home

A 10:20 AM international departure means a disciplined morning — here's the exact clock to follow.

⏱️ The Timeline

6:00 AM

⏰ Wake, final pack, check out

Pack the night before. Leave the key per host instructions and photograph the room (deposit protection).

6:30 AM

🚶 Leave the Airbnb → Taipei Main Station

From Ximending it's a 10-min walk or one MRT stop to Taipei Main. Follow the purple Airport MRT (A1) signs — the terminal is west of the main hall (allow 10 min of walking inside the station). Bonus: many airlines offer in-town check-in at A1 (bag drop up to 3 hrs before departure) — if yours participates, drop bags here and fly through the airport.

7:00 AM

🚄 Airport MRT Express to Terminal

Take the purple Express train (not Commuter) — 36–39 min to the terminals, every ~15 min from ~6:00 AM. NT$150 with EasyCard. You'll arrive at the airport ~7:40 AM.

💰 NT$150/pax⏱️ 36–39 min🚇 Timetable
7:45 AM

🛄 Airport check-in — 2.5 hrs before

Counters for a 10:20 flight open around 7:20–7:50. Check in, clear security and immigration by ~8:45, leaving a relaxed 60+ minutes at the gate.

8:30 AM

🥡 Breakfast options (pick one)

Fastest: grab a fan tuan (sticky rice roll) + soy milk from the breakfast shops near Taipei Main before boarding the MRT. Airside: Taoyuan's food courts do decent beef noodles and Din Tai Fung-style dumplings post-security. Last pasalubong: Chia Te and SunnyHills pineapple cakes are sold in the departure halls — buy here if you skipped them in town.

💰 NT$100–250/pax
10:20 AM

🛫 Wheels up — TPE → MNL

Land in Manila 1:05 PM with an EasyCard souvenir, a camera roll full of lanterns, and (statistically) 1.5 kg of pineapple cake.

🚨
Fallback: if you somehow miss the ~7:15 Express, a taxi from Ximending to TPE is 40–50 min and NT$1,100–1,300 — expensive but flight-saving. Don't cut it closer than a 7:30 AM train.
