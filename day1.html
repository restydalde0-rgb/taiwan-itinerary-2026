Arrival Logistics

The 2:00 AM Question

The Airport MRT doesn't run overnight (roughly 6:00 AM – midnight), so you have three realistic moves:

✅ Recommended

🚌 Night bus + check in immediately

Take the 24-hour Kuo-Kuang Bus 1819 (, every 30–60 min overnight, ~55 min) from the arrivals hall to Taipei Main Station, then a 10-minute walk or short taxi to your stay. Book your accommodation from the night of Aug 17 so you can walk straight in at 3:30 AM. One "extra" night costs less than a wasted, zombie first day.

🛋️ Wait at the airport until ~6 AM

Taoyuan T1/T2 are safe with 24-hr convenience stores and rest zones. Catch the first Airport MRT Express (from ~6:00 AM, , 36 min). Free savings, but you'll start Day 1 exhausted — only worth it if you sleep well on planes.

🧳 Luggage storage if check-in is 3 PM

If early check-in truly isn't possible: coin lockers at Taipei Main Station B1 () or the airport's luggage counters, then start the day light. Solid plan B, but plan A beats it.

The Timeline

2:00 AM

🛬 Land, clear immigration, ride into the city

Immigration at 2 AM is fast (~20–30 min). Grab cash at the arrivals ATM, activate your eSIM, and buy an EasyCard at the 24-hr convenience store (card + load each).

🚌 Airport → Home base
  1. 🛬2:45 AM — follow the Bus signs to the arrivals-level bus bays; buy 1819 tickets at the Kuo-Kuang counter (24 hrs).
  2. 🚌~3:00 AM — board Bus 1819 toward Taipei Main Station. Ride ~55 min.
  3. 🚏~3:55 AM — alight at Taipei Main Station (West bus terminal).
  4. 🚶Walk ~10 min to the apartment (or a metered taxi, ~ per car, if bags are heavy).

4:00 – 10:00 AM

😴 Check in & sleep — protect the trip

Self check-in, blackout curtains, alarm at 10:00. Skipping this sleep is the #1 first-timer mistake on red-eye arrivals. No schedule guilt: today officially starts at brunch.

10:45 AM 🚪 Leave apartment

🍳 Brunch: Fuhang Dou Jiang Iconic

Taiwan's most famous breakfast house (Huashan Market, 2F). Charcoal-toasted shaobing with egg, warm salty soy milk, youtiao. Why it's worth it: this is the definitive version of the Taiwanese breakfast — the queue is part of the show. Late morning is much calmer than the 7 AM rush. Closed Mondays — you're safe on a Tuesday.

Backup with zero queue: Yonghe Doujiang Dawang, any branch.

12:15 PM 🚪 Leave Fuhang

🎪 Ximending on foot — murals, hidden lanes & the Red House Your neighborhood

Why this gets the whole afternoon: Ximending isn't one sight — it's a dozen small worlds packed into six pedestrian blocks, and it's literally where you sleep. Walk it as a loop, unhurried:

🚶 The loop: start at the rainbow crossing (Ximen Exit 6 — the district's most-photographed square meter), drift up Cinema Street for the movie murals and street art, duck into Tattoo Street (Hanzhong St Lane 50 — neon, ink parlors, great gritty photos), then American Street (Kunming St 96 alley) for graffiti walls, vintage clothing and second-hand streetwear. Finish at the octagonal 1908 Red House — a heritage theater turned designer-craft market (free entry, closed Mondays).

🍢 Graze as you go: Ay-Chung Flour-Rice Noodle (everyone eats standing — join them, ), Hot-Star XXL crispy chicken at its original branch (), and Snow King (since 1947) for one scoop of something strange — soy sauce and chili ice cream are the classics.

📸 Photo spots: rainbow crossing from the Exit 6 corner · Cinema Street murals · American Street graffiti · Red House façade at dusk. 🎤 Street performers set up around the pedestrian zone from late afternoon — stop and watch, that's the point of today.

2:15 PM

☕ Coffee break: Fong Da Coffee, since 1956 Sit down, slow down

A Ximending institution — order the iced coffee and a bag of their butter-walnut cookies, take a table, and do nothing for 45 minutes. This break is a scheduled itinerary item, not filler: it resets your feet for the evening and parks you indoors during the usual 2–4 PM thunderstorm window. Third-wave alternative one block away: 8¾ Coffee.

💰 📍 Google Maps
3:00 PM

🕹️ Shop & play: Donki, Wannian, Animate & the arcades

The indoor half of Ximending — all within three blocks of each other, all air-conditioned:

🛒 Don Don Donki (open 24 hours) — Japan's chaos emporium: snacks, skincare, weird gadgets and half your pasalubong list in one basement. · 🏢 Wannian Commercial Building — six floors of sneaker shops, anime figures, capsule-toy walls and a top-floor arcade with claw machines (budget of coins, lose them happily). · 🎌 Animate Taipei — the anime & manga megastore. · 👟 Sneaker row along Wuchang Street and the vintage racks back on American Street if something caught your eye earlier.

💡 No pressure to finish: you live here for three more nights — anything you skip is a five-minute walk away any evening of the trip.

4:45 PM 🚪 Walk home — 5 min

😌 Real rest: drop the bags, shower, feet up

Scheduled on purpose. This is the payoff of sleeping inside Ximending: drop your shopping, cool off, nap if you want. Tonight is the trip's one big night-market crawl — arrive at it fresh, not spent. No guilt, no checklist.

🏮 Optional, if you're restless: Longshan Temple is one Blue Line stop away — incense and evening chanting from ~5:30 PM are genuinely atmospheric. Only go if your legs vote yes.

5:55 PM 🚪 Leave apartment

🏮 Raohe Street Night Market Must-eat

Why visit: one 600-meter lantern-lit lane beside the gorgeous Ciyou Temple. Eat your way down: Fuzhou Shizu pepper pork bun (Michelin Bib Gourmand, at the temple-side entrance, ), medicinal pork-rib soup, grilled king oyster mushrooms, sweet potato balls, mochi.

📸 Photo spot: the neon market gate with Ciyou Temple's roof dragons behind it.

  4. 🏮~6:30 — start at the temple gate; eat down the single lane at your own pace.

8:45 PM

🌉 Rainbow Bridge — a riverside night walk

Why finish here: three minutes behind Ciyou Temple, the S-curved, LED-lit Rainbow Bridge arcs over the Keelung River — river breeze, city lights on the water, and almost no tourists. Cross it slowly, sit on the riverside steps, digest the market. This is the calm counterpoint to the crawl you just did.

📸 Photo spots: the red S-curve of the bridge lit against the black river · the LOVE installation at the bridgehead · Ciyou Temple's glowing roof from the far bank.
