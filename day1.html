Landing Softly: Ximending, Memorials & Raohe Lights

A smart 2 AM arrival plan, a slow brunch morning, then Taipei's classics — ending under the lanterns of its best night market.

🌙 The 2:00 AM Question

The Airport MRT doesn't run overnight (roughly 6:00 AM – midnight), so you have three realistic moves:

✅ Recommended

🚌 Night bus + check in immediately

Take the 24-hour Kuo-Kuang Bus 1819 (NT$140, every 30–60 min overnight, ~50 min) from the arrivals hall to Taipei Main Station, then a short taxi/walk to your stay. Book your accommodation from the night of Aug 17 (or confirm free 3 AM check-in with the host). You'll be asleep by ~4 AM and wake up on your own clock. One "extra" night costs less than a wasted, zombie first day.

🛋️ Wait at the airport until ~6 AM

Taoyuan T1/T2 are safe with 24-hr convenience stores and rest zones. Catch the first Airport MRT Express (from ~6:00 AM, NT$150, 36 min). Free, but you'll start Day 1 exhausted — only worth it if you sleep well on planes.

🧳 Luggage storage if check-in is 3 PM

If early check-in truly isn't possible: drop bags in the coin lockers at Taipei Main Station B1 (NT$20–70/3 hrs) or the airport's luggage counters, then start the day light. Solid plan B, but plan A beats it.

⏱️ The Timeline

2:00 – 4:00 AM

🛬 Land, clear immigration, ride into the city

Immigration at 2 AM is fast (~20–30 min). Grab cash at the arrivals ATM, pick up your pre-ordered SIM/eSIM, and buy an EasyCard at the 24-hr convenience store. Board Bus 1819 to Taipei Main Station.

💰 Bus NT$140/pax⏱️ ~50 min📍 Map
4:00 – 10:00 AM

😴 Check in & sleep — protect the trip

Self check-in, blackout curtains, alarm at 10:00. Skipping this sleep is the #1 first-timer mistake on red-eye arrivals.

10:45 AM

🍳 Brunch: Fuhang Dou Jiang Iconic

Taiwan's most famous breakfast house (Huashan Market, 2F). Charcoal-toasted shaobing with egg, warm salty soy milk, youtiao. The queue moves fast; late morning is calmer than 7 AM. Closed Mondays — you're safe on a Tuesday.

Backup with zero queue: Yonghe Doujiang Dawang, any branch.

🚇 Ximen → Shandao Temple (Blue line, 2 stops)💰 ~NT$120/pax📍 Map
12:15 PM

🎪 Ximending + The Red House

Back to home turf: Asia's "Harajuku of Taipei." Wander the pedestrian grid, Cinema Street murals, sneaker shops and claw machines. Then step into the octagonal 1908 Red House — heritage theater turned designer-craft market (free entry, closed Mondays).

Café stop: 8¾ or Fong Da Coffee (since 1956) for an iced brew.

🚇 Shandao Temple → Ximen (Blue line)💰 Free to wander📍 Map
2:45 PM

🏛️ Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall

Taipei's grandest plaza: the white-and-blue octagonal hall, Liberty Square arch, and the National Theater & Concert Hall flanking it. Time your visit for the top-of-the-hour honor guard changing ceremony (now held in the outdoor plaza area, hourly until 5 PM). Free entry.

📸 Photo spot: shoot the arch framing the hall from dead center of the square; puddle reflections after rain are a bonus.

🚇 Ximen → CKS Memorial Hall (Green line, 2 stops)💰 Free⏱️ 60–75 min📍 Map
5:00 PM

🌇 Taipei 101 at golden hour Sunset ~6:25 PM

Ride to Taipei 101/World Trade Center station. Coffee or window-shop the luxury mall, then catch golden hour around the tower — the classic angle is from Xinyi Place / ATT 4 FUN side, or the LOVE sculpture corner. (You'll go up the tower tomorrow; today is for shooting it from below.)

🚇 CKS Memorial Hall → Taipei 101 (Red line direct, 7 stops)💰 Free📍 Map
7:15 PM

🏮 Raohe Street Night Market Must-eat

One 600-meter lantern-lit lane beside the gorgeous Ciyou Temple — the most photogenic night market in Taipei. Eat your way down: Fuzhou Shizu pepper pork bun (Michelin Bib Gourmand, right at the temple-side entrance, ~NT$60), medicinal pork rib soup, grilled king oyster mushrooms, sweet potato balls, mochi.

📸 Photo spot: the neon market gate with Ciyou Temple's roof dragons behind it.

🚇 Taipei 101 → Daan → Songshan (transfer Red→Green... simplest: bus BL5 / taxi 15 min)💰 NT$300–400 food budget📍 Map
9:45 PM

🌙 MRT home from Songshan

Songshan station (Green line) is at the market's east end — one train straight back to Ximen, no transfers. Sleep well; tomorrow starts with a hike.

🚇 Songshan → Ximen (Green line direct, ~20 min)
