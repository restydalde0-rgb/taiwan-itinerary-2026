Landing Softly: Ximending, Memorials & Raohe Lights

A smart 2 AM arrival plan, a slow brunch morning, then Taipei's classics at an unhurried pace — ending under the lanterns of its best night market.

Why the day is shaped this way: after a 2 AM landing you sleep first — that protects all three sightseeing days. The afternoon then moves in one straight line east: home base (Ximending) → CKS Memorial (2 green-line stops) → Taipei 101 (one red-line ride) → Raohe (short taxi hop) → one direct green-line train home. Arrival Logistics

The 2:00 AM Question

The Airport MRT doesn't run overnight (roughly 6:00 AM – midnight), so you have three realistic moves:

✅ Recommended

🚌 Night bus + check in immediately

Take the 24-hour Kuo-Kuang Bus 1819 (, every 30–60 min overnight, ~55 min) from the arrivals hall to Taipei Main Station, then a 10-minute walk or short taxi to your stay. Book your accommodation from the night of Aug 17 so you can walk straight in at 3:30 AM. One "extra" night costs less than a wasted, zombie first day.

🛋️ Wait at the airport until ~6 AM

Taoyuan T1/T2 are safe with 24-hr convenience stores and rest zones. Catch the first Airport MRT Express (from ~6:00 AM, , 36 min). Free savings, but you'll start Day 1 exhausted — only worth it if you sleep well on planes.

🧳 Luggage storage if check-in is 3 PM

If early check-in truly isn't possible: coin lockers at Taipei Main Station B1 () or the airport's luggage counters, then start the day light. Solid plan B, but plan A beats it.

Hour by Hour

The Timeline

2:00 AM

🛬 Land, clear immigration, ride into the city

Immigration at 2 AM is fast (~20–30 min). Grab cash at the arrivals ATM, activate your eSIM, and buy an EasyCard at the 24-hr convenience store (card + load each).

🚌 Airport → Home base
  1. 🛬2:45 AM — follow the Bus signs to the arrivals-level bus bays; buy 1819 tickets at the Kuo-Kuang counter (24 hrs).
  2. 🚌~3:00 AM — board Bus 1819 toward Taipei Main Station. Ride ~55 min.
  3. 🚏~3:55 AM — alight at Taipei Main Station (West bus terminal).
  4. 🚶Walk ~10 min to the apartment (or a metered taxi, ~ per car, if bags are heavy).
⏱️ Est. total: ~70 min door-to-door💰 Fare: 📏 ~43 km
📍 Google Maps
4:00 – 10:00 AM

😴 Check in & sleep — protect the trip

Self check-in, blackout curtains, alarm at 10:00. Skipping this sleep is the #1 first-timer mistake on red-eye arrivals. No schedule guilt: today officially starts at brunch.

10:45 AM 🚪 Leave apartment

🍳 Brunch: Fuhang Dou Jiang Iconic

Taiwan's most famous breakfast house (Huashan Market, 2F). Charcoal-toasted shaobing with egg, warm salty soy milk, youtiao. Why it's worth it: this is the definitive version of the Taiwanese breakfast — the queue is part of the show. Late morning is much calmer than the 7 AM rush. Closed Mondays — you're safe on a Tuesday.

Backup with zero queue: Yonghe Doujiang Dawang, any branch.

🚇 Apartment → Fuhang Dou Jiang
  1. 🚶10:45 — walk 6 min to Ximen Station.
  2. 🚇10:53 — board the Blue Line toward Nangang. Ride 2 stations.
  3. 🚉10:58 — get off at Shandao Temple, take Exit 5.
  4. 🚶Walk 3 min to Huashan Market; Fuhang is on the 2nd floor. Arrive ~11:05.

12:15 PM 🚪 Leave Fuhang

🎪 Ximending + The Red House

Why visit: Asia's liveliest pedestrian grid is literally your neighborhood — street fashion, Cinema Street murals, sneaker shops and claw machines. Then step into the octagonal 1908 Red House — heritage theater turned designer-craft market (free entry, closed Mondays). Since you sleep next door, there's zero pressure to see it all today.

☕ Built-in coffee break (1:45 PM): Fong Da Coffee (since 1956) for an iced brew, or 8¾ if you want third-wave. Sit, cool down, let your feet recover before the afternoon.

🚇 Fuhang → Ximending
  1. 🚇12:20 — Blue Line toward Dingpu, 2 stations back to Ximen, Exit 6.
  2. 🚶Exit 6 lands you at the famous rainbow crossing — you're there. Arrive ~12:35.

3:00 PM 🚪 Leave Ximending

🏛️ Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall

Why visit: Taipei's grandest plaza — the octagonal hall, Liberty Square arch, and the National Theater & Concert Hall flanking it. Aim for the 4:00 PM honor-guard ceremony (top of every hour until 5 PM, held in the outdoor plaza area). The 3–4 PM slot also parks you somewhere shaded/indoor during the typical afternoon-thunderstorm window.

📸 Photo spots: shoot the arch framing the hall from dead center of the square; puddle reflections after rain are a bonus.

🚇 Ximen → CKS Memorial Hall
  1. 🚶3:00 — walk to Ximen Station (~4 min).
  2. 🚇3:06 — board the Green Line toward Xindian. Ride 2 stations.
  3. 🚉3:11 — alight at Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall, take Exit 5 — it opens onto Liberty Square. Arrive ~3:15.

4:45 PM 🚪 Leave CKS

🌇 Taipei 101 at golden hour Sunset ~6:25 PM

Why visit now: golden hour paints the tower and the Xinyi district skybridges. Cool off in the 101 mall first (AC + window shopping + a drink), then head out for the light. You'll ride the observatory tomorrow — today is for shooting the tower from below.

📸 Photo spots: Xinyi Place / ATT 4 FUN skybridge side, and the LOVE sculpture corner.

6:50 PM 🚪 Leave Taipei 101

🏮 Raohe Street Night Market Must-eat

Why visit: one 600-meter lantern-lit lane beside the gorgeous Ciyou Temple. Eat your way down: Fuzhou Shizu pepper pork bun (Michelin Bib Gourmand, at the temple-side entrance, ), medicinal pork-rib soup, grilled king oyster mushrooms, sweet potato balls, mochi.

📸 Photo spot: the neon market gate with Ciyou Temple's roof dragons behind it.

🚕 Taipei 101 → Raohe (taxi recommended)
  1. 🚕6:50 — hail a taxi or Uber at the tower rank. Ride ~15 min to the Ciyou Temple end of Raohe. ~.
  2. 🏮~7:10 — start at the temple gate; eat down the single lane at your own pace.
⏱️ Est. total: ~15–20 min💰 Split two ways: 📏 ~4 km
