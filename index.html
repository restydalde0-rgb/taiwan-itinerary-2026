Day 01 · Tuesday, 18 August

Landing Softly: Ximending, Memorials & Raohe Lights

A smart 2 AM arrival plan, a slow brunch morning, then Taipei's classics — ending under the lanterns of its best night market.

🛬 Arrival day🏛️ Icons🌆 Sunset at 101🏮 Raohe Night Market

🌙 The 2:00 AM Question

The Airport MRT doesn't run overnight (roughly 6:00 AM – midnight), so you have three realistic moves:

✅ Recommended

🚌 Night bus + check in immediately

Take the 24-hour Kuo-Kuang Bus 1819 (NT$140, every 30–60 min overnight, ~50 min) from the arrivals hall to Taipei Main Station, then a short taxi/walk to your stay. Book your accommodation from the night of Aug 17 (or confirm free 3 AM check-in with the host). You'll be asleep by ~4 AM and wake up on your own clock. One "extra" night costs less than a wasted, zombie first day.

🛋️ Wait at the airport until ~6 AM

Taoyuan T1/T2 are safe with 24-hr convenience stores and rest zones. Catch the first Airport MRT Express (from ~6:00 AM, NT$150, 36 min). Free, but you'll start Day 1 exhausted — only worth it if you sleep well on planes.

🧳 Luggage storage if check-in is 3 PM

If early check-in truly isn't possible: drop bags in the coin lockers at Taipei Main Station B1 (NT$20–70/3 hrs) or the airport's luggage counters, then start the day light. Solid plan B, but plan A beats it.

2:00 – 4:00 AM

🛬 Land, clear immigration, ride into the city

Immigration at 2 AM is fast (~20–30 min). Grab cash at the arrivals ATM, pick up your pre-ordered SIM/eSIM, and buy an EasyCard at the 24-hr convenience store. Board Bus 1819 to Taipei Main Station.

💰 Bus NT$140/pax⏱️ ~50 min📍 Map
4:00 – 10:00 AM

😴 Check in & sleep — protect the trip

Self check-in, blackout curtains, alarm at 10:00. Skipping this sleep is the #1 first-timer mistake on red-eye arrivals.

10:45 AM

🍳 Brunch: Fuhang Dou Jiang Iconic

Taiwan's most famous breakfast house (Huashan Market, 2F). Charcoal-toasted shaobing with egg, warm salty soy milk, youtiao. The queue moves fast; late morning is calmer than 7 AM. Closed Mondays — you're safe on a Tuesday.

Backup with zero queue: Yonghe Doujiang Dawang, any branch.

🚇 Ximen → Shandao Temple (Blue line, 2 stops)💰 ~NT$120/pax📍 Map
12:15 PM

🎪 Ximending + The Red House

Back to home turf: Asia's "Harajuku of Taipei." Wander the pedestrian grid, Cinema Street murals, sneaker shops and claw machines. Then step into the octagonal 1908 Red House — heritage theater turned designer-craft market (free entry, closed Mondays).

Café stop: 8¾ or Fong Da Coffee (since 1956) for an iced brew.

🚇 Shandao Temple → Ximen (Blue line)💰 Free to wander📍 Map
2:45 PM

🏛️ Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall

Taipei's grandest plaza: the white-and-blue octagonal hall, Liberty Square arch, and the National Theater & Concert Hall flanking it. Time your visit for the top-of-the-hour honor guard changing ceremony (now held in the outdoor plaza area, hourly until 5 PM). Free entry.

📸 Photo spot: shoot the arch framing the hall from dead center of the square; puddle reflections after rain are a bonus.

🚇 Ximen → CKS Memorial Hall (Green line, 2 stops)💰 Free⏱️ 60–75 min📍 Map
5:00 PM

🌇 Taipei 101 at golden hour Sunset ~6:25 PM

Ride to Taipei 101/World Trade Center station. Coffee or window-shop the luxury mall, then catch golden hour around the tower — the classic angle is from Xinyi Place / ATT 4 FUN side, or the LOVE sculpture corner. (You'll go up the tower tomorrow; today is for shooting it from below.)

🚇 CKS Memorial Hall → Taipei 101 (Red line direct, 7 stops)💰 Free📍 Map
7:15 PM

🏮 Raohe Street Night Market Must-eat

One 600-meter lantern-lit lane beside the gorgeous Ciyou Temple — the most photogenic night market in Taipei. Eat your way down: Fuzhou Shizu pepper pork bun (Michelin Bib Gourmand, right at the temple-side entrance, ~NT$60), medicinal pork rib soup, grilled king oyster mushrooms, sweet potato balls, mochi.

📸 Photo spot: the neon market gate with Ciyou Temple's roof dragons behind it.

🚇 Taipei 101 → Daan → Songshan (transfer Red→Green... simplest: bus BL5 / taxi 15 min)💰 NT$300–400 food budget📍 Map
9:45 PM

🌙 MRT home from Songshan

Songshan station (Green line) is at the market's east end — one train straight back to Ximen, no transfers. Sleep well; tomorrow starts with a hike.

🚇 Songshan → Ximen (Green line direct, ~20 min)
📍 Day 1 finale: Raohe Street Night Market & Ciyou Temple
Day 02 · Wednesday, 19 August

The Classics: Elephant Mountain to Ningxia Nights

Taipei's postcard day — the skyline from above (twice), a creative park for café lovers, and the city's best old-school eating street.

⛰️ Sunrise-ish hike🏙️ 101 Observatory🎨 Huashan 1914🍢 Ningxia Night Market
7:30 AM

⛰️ Elephant Mountain (Xiangshan) Hidden-gem hour

Go early — August heat is brutal by 10 AM. It's ~600 steep stone steps, 20–25 minutes to the famous six giant boulders with the head-on Taipei 101 view. Bring water; there are shaded rest platforms.

📸 Photo spot: climb onto the boulders for the classic "sitting above the skyline" shot — morning light hits the tower's face.

🚇 Ximen → Xiangshan (Green→Red at CKS... simplest: Blue line to Taipei City Hall + Red 1 stop; or door-to-door ~30 min)💰 Free⏱️ 2 hrs round trip📍 Map
10:15 AM

🏯 Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall

One MRT stop away. A sweeping Tang-style roof, hourly honor-guard ceremony, and the other famous Taipei 101 angle — the tower rising over the memorial's eaves from the front lawn. Air-conditioned exhibits inside are a welcome heat break.

🚇 Xiangshan → Taipei 101 → walk, or Red line + 10-min walk💰 Free📍 Map
11:45 AM

🍜 Lunch at Din Tai Fung, Taipei 101 branch Bucket list

The Michelin-starred home of xiao long bao, in the 101 mall basement. Get a queue number first, browse the mall, then feast: 10 pork XLB (~NT$250), truffle XLB if you're celebrating, shrimp fried rice. ~NT$500–650/person.

💰 ~NT$1,100 for two📍 Map
1:30 PM

🔭 Taipei 101 Observatory — worth it? Verdict: yes, if clear

Honest take: at NT$600 it's worth it on a clear day — the 37-second elevator, the 89F panorama and the massive gold tuned-mass damper are genuinely cool. If it's hazy or stormy, skip it; your Elephant Mountain photos this morning already beat a cloudy window. Decide at the ticket desk by looking up.

Skip-the-crowd tip: weekday early afternoon (right now) has the shortest lines. Skyline 460 (101F open-air) is NT$980 — only for enthusiasts.

💰 NT$600/pax⏱️ 60–90 min🎫 Official tickets
3:45 PM

🎨 Huashan 1914 Creative Park Café heaven

A 1914 wine factory reborn as Taipei's coolest creative village — indie design shops, exhibitions, ivy-covered brick walls. Perfect café stop: Fika Fika Café (Nordic roast champions, 5 min away) or the park's own courtyard cafés.

📸 Photo spot: the ivy wall and smokestack alley on the park's east side.

🚇 Taipei 101 → Zhongxiao Xinsheng (Red→Blue at Daan, ~20 min)💰 Free entry📍 Map
6:30 PM

🍢 Ningxia Night Market Locals' favorite

Small, dense, and ranked by Taipei residents as the city's tastiest market. Non-negotiables: oyster omelet (Yuan Huan Pien, ~NT$80), Liu Yu Zai deep-fried taro balls (Bib Gourmand, NT$50), chicken rice, papaya milk. Arrive at opening to beat lines.

🚇 Zhongxiao Xinsheng → Zhongshan (Green line, then 10-min walk)💰 NT$300–400 food budget📍 Map
8:45 PM

☕ Optional nightcap: Zhongshan's café lane

You're beside Chifeng Street — vintage shops and late cafés. Or head straight home: tomorrow is the early day trip.

🚇 Zhongshan → Ximen (Green line direct, 2 stops)
📍 Day 2 opener: Xiangshan (Elephant Mountain) hiking trail
Day 03 · Thursday, 20 August

Spirited Away: Shifen Lanterns & Jiufen Tea Houses

The unmissable day trip — release a sky lantern on old railway tracks, chase a waterfall, then sip tea above the sea in a lantern-lit mountain town.

🚂 Pingxi Line🏮 Sky lantern💦 Shifen Waterfall🍵 A-Mei Tea House
🧭
Route logic: Shifen first, Jiufen second. Shifen Waterfall closes ~5 PM, while Jiufen is at its magical best when the red lanterns glow from ~5:30 PM. Doing it in this order means no backtracking and both places at their peak.
7:45 AM

🚆 TRA train: Taipei Main → Ruifang

From Taipei Main Station's TRA level (not MRT), tap in with EasyCard on any northbound local/express train toward Su'ao or Hualien. Express ~40 min (NT$76), local ~50 min (NT$49). Aim for a departure between 7:45–8:15.

💰 NT$49–76/pax⏱️ 40–50 min🎫 TRA timetable
9:00 AM

🚂 Pingxi Line: Ruifang → Shifen

Transfer at Ruifang to the one-track heritage Pingxi Line (trains roughly hourly — check the return times posted on the platform and photograph the timetable). A gorgeous 30-minute river-gorge ride.

💰 NT$20 (or NT$80 day pass)⏱️ 30 min📍 Map
9:40 AM

🏮 Shifen Old Street — release your sky lantern Trip highlight

Shops line the live railway track. Pick a lantern color by wish (red = luck, pink = love, yellow = money — a 4-color lantern covers everything, NT$200–250). Paint your wishes with brush ink, then the shop lights it and photographs the release on the tracks for you — they're pros at this.

📸 Photo spot: lantern rising with the old street receding behind; and the suspension bridge over Keelung River. Step off when the train bell rings!

💰 NT$200–250/lantern⏱️ ~1 hr with snacks
11:00 AM

💦 Shifen Waterfall — "Little Niagara of Taiwan"

A shaded 20–25 min walk from the old street (or NT$50 shuttle). The 40-m-wide cascade curtain is Taiwan's broadest waterfall; viewing platforms give three angles. Free entry. Grab a grilled sausage or peanut ice cream roll from the stalls on the way back.

💰 Free⏱️ 1.5 hrs round trip📍 Map
1:15 PM

🚌 Shifen → Jiufen (via Ruifang)

Pingxi Line back to Ruifang (NT$20, 30 min), then bus 788 or 825/827 from the stop across Ruifang Station up the mountain to Jiufen Old Street (NT$15, 15–20 min). Shortcut option: fixed-fare taxi Shifen→Jiufen direct, NT$800–1,000 per car — worth it if a train is 40+ minutes away.

💰 NT$35 by rail+bus⏱️ ~1 hr total
2:45 PM

⛰️ Jiufen Old Street — eat first, shop deep

Enter at the 7-Eleven mouth of Jishan Street and graze: Grandma Lai's taro balls (warm or iced, NT$50 — eat them on the terrace overlooking the sea), A-Zhu peanut ice cream roll (NT$50), fish balls, ocarina shops, tea sets. The alleys reward wandering.

💰 NT$200–300 grazing📍 Map
4:30 PM

🍵 A-Mei Tea House — golden hour tea Iconic

The lantern-covered tea house on Shuqi Road that every photo of Jiufen features. A traditional gongfu tea set with mountain-and-sea views runs ~NT$300/person (tea leaves take-home included) — settle onto the terrace and stay as the lanterns switch on around 5:30. Quieter alternative: Jiufen Teahouse or Siidcha.

📸 Photo spots: (1) the Shuqi Road stone stairs facing A-Mei's lantern wall at blue hour — the "Spirited Away" shot; (2) the sea-view balcony at Shengping Theater.

💰 ~NT$600 for two📍 Map
6:45 PM

🚌 Down the mountain: bus 1062 straight to Taipei

Board the Keelung Bus 1062 from Jiufen's main road directly to Zhongxiao Fuxing MRT in Taipei (NT$102 with EasyCard, ~70–80 min). Get in line early — evening queues form. Alternative: bus 788 down to Ruifang + TRA train back.

💰 NT$102/pax⏱️ ~75 min
8:30 PM

🍲 Late dinner back in Taipei: beef noodles

You'll land at Zhongxiao Fuxing — perfect for Lin Dong Fang beef noodles (open till late, NT$180–260 a bowl, add the housemade chili butter). Then Blue line home to Ximen.

🚇 Zhongxiao Fuxing → Ximen (Blue line direct)💰 ~NT$500 for two📍 Map

One Last Breakfast, Then Home

A 10:20 AM international departure means a disciplined morning — here's the exact clock to follow.

🛫 TPE 10:20 AM🚄 Airport MRT Express🥐 Breakfast plan
6:00 AM

⏰ Wake, final pack, check out

Pack the night before. Leave the key per host instructions and photograph the room (deposit protection).

6:30 AM

🚶 Leave the Airbnb → Taipei Main Station

From Ximending it's a 10-min walk or one MRT stop to Taipei Main. Follow the purple Airport MRT (A1) signs — the terminal is west of the main hall (allow 10 min of walking inside the station). Airside: Taoyuan's food courts do decent beef noodles and Din Tai Fung-style dumplings post-security. Getting Around

Transport, Decoded

Taipei has one of Asia's cleanest, easiest transit systems. One card runs everything.

💳

EasyCard (悠遊卡)

  • Buy at any airport/MRT convenience store: NT$100 for the card + top-up (start with NT$500 each).
  • Taps onto MRT, buses, TRA trains (incl. Ruifang!), YouBike and convenience stores.
  • 20% off MRT fares vs single tickets + free bus↔MRT transfer discounts.
  • Top up in cash at any 7-Eleven or station machine.
🚄

Airport MRT

  • Express train: Taipei Main ↔ terminals in 36–39 min, NT$150.
  • Runs ~6:00 AM – 11:00 PM+ (not overnight — hence the Day 1 night-bus plan).
  • Purple line; Express has plush seats & luggage racks.
  • In-town check-in for select airlines at A1 Taipei Main.
🚇

Taipei MRT

  • Fares NT$20–65 by distance; this itinerary averages NT$25/ride.
  • Lines are color-coded — you'll mostly use Blue (Bannan), Green (Songshan-Xindian) and Red (Tamsui-Xinyi).
  • Runs 6:00 AM – midnight, trains every 3–7 min.
  • No eating or drinking past the fare gates (fines are real!).
🚌

Buses & Day-trip Rail

  • City buses NT$15/section, tap on and off with EasyCard.
  • Bus 1819: 24-hr airport ↔ Taipei Main, NT$140.
  • Day 3: TRA to Ruifang NT$49–76 · Pingxi Line NT$20 · bus to Jiufen NT$15 · bus 1062 home NT$102.
  • Google Maps transit directions are excellent in Taipei.
💰
Estimated total transport spend: ≈ NT$1,250 per person (₱2,410) for the whole trip — airport transfers NT$290, three days of MRT/bus ~NT$450, and the full Jiufen/Shifen loop ~NT$250, plus buffer. Load NT$600 onto each EasyCard on arrival and top up once mid-trip.
