Spirited Away: Shifen Lanterns & Jiufen Tea Houses

The unmissable day trip — release a sky lantern on old railway tracks, chase a waterfall, then sip tea above the sea in a lantern-lit mountain town.

Why Shifen first, Jiufen second: Shifen Waterfall's park closes ~5 PM, while Jiufen is at its magical best when the red lanterns glow from ~5:30 PM. This order hits both at their peak with zero backtracking — and the tea house doubles as your built-in afternoon rest. The travel itself (river-gorge heritage railway) is one of the day's highlights, not dead time.

⏰ Wake + station breakfast

The one early alarm of the trip — and the one that's worth it: Shifen before the tour buses is a different town, and the waterfall park closes at 5 PM, so the whole day only works front-loaded. Grab a fan tuan (sticky-rice roll) and soy milk from the breakfast shops around Taipei Main (~) — or convenience-store onigiri to eat on the platform (eating is fine on TRA trains, unlike the MRT).

🚂 Pingxi Line: Ruifang → Shifen

Transfer at Ruifang to the one-track heritage Pingxi Line (trains roughly hourly). A gorgeous 30-minute river-gorge ride — sit on the right side going in for the Keelung River views.

⏱️ ~30 min ride💰 (or day pass)
~9:45 AM

🏮 Shifen Old Street — release your sky lantern Trip highlight

Why visit: lantern shops line a live railway track — this is the wish-on-a-lantern scene you came to Taiwan for. Pick a color by wish (red = luck, pink = love, yellow = money; a 4-color lantern covers everything, ). Paint your wishes in brush ink, then the shop lights it and photographs the release on the tracks for you — they're pros at this.

📸 Photo spots: lantern rising with the old street receding behind; the suspension bridge over the Keelung River. Step off when the train bell rings!

11:00 AM 🚶 Walk from Old Street

💦 Shifen Waterfall — "Little Niagara of Taiwan"

Why visit: a 40-meter-wide cascade curtain — Taiwan's broadest waterfall, 20–25 minutes' shaded walk from the old street (or a shuttle). Three viewing platforms give three angles. Free entry.

🌭 Lunch on the way back (~12:30): the stall cluster by the trail does grilled sausage, chicken-wing rice and peanut ice-cream rolls — this is today's lunch (~).

~2:30 PM

⛰️ Jiufen Old Street — eat first, shop deep

Why visit: a 1890s gold-rush town stacked on a cliff over the Pacific, all narrow lanes, tea houses and red lanterns. Enter at the 7-Eleven mouth of Jishan Street and graze: Grandma Lai's taro balls (warm or iced, — eat them on the terrace overlooking the sea), A-Zhu peanut ice-cream roll (), fish balls, ocarina shops, tea sets. The side alleys reward wandering — this 2-hour block is deliberately unscheduled.

4:30 PM

🍵 A-Mei Tea House — golden hour tea Iconic

Why visit: the lantern-covered tea house on Shuqi Road that every photo of Jiufen features. A traditional gongfu tea set with mountain-and-sea views runs ~ (take-home tea leaves included). Settle onto the terrace and stay put as the lanterns switch on around 5:30 — this double-duties as your afternoon rest. Quieter alternatives: Jiufen Teahouse or Siidcha.

📸 Photo spots: (1) the Shuqi Road stone stairs facing A-Mei's lantern wall at blue hour — the "Spirited Away" shot; (2) the sea-view balcony at Shengping Theater.

6:15 PM 🚪 Leave Jiufen

🚌 Down the mountain: bus 1062 straight to Taipei

🚌 Jiufen → Zhongxiao Fuxing, Taipei
  1. 🚏6:15 — walk to the 1062 stop on Jiufen's main road and queue early — evening lines form fast.
  2. 🚌~6:30 — board Keelung Bus 1062 direct to Taipei. Ride ~75 min. .
  3. 🚉~7:50 — alight at Zhongxiao Fuxing MRT — dinner is a 3-min walk away.
⏱️ Est. total: ~90 min💰 Fare: 📏 ~35 km
Why the bus beats the train home: the rail route (bus down to Ruifang + TRA + MRT) needs three legs and ~30 extra minutes. The 1062 is one seat all the way into the city, and it drops you at the door of tonight's dinner.
