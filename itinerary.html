Day by Day

Four Days, Never Rushed

Each day owns one part of the city, moves in one direction, and has rest built in — slow mornings, a coffee or tea break every afternoon, and a different kind of evening every night. Tap any day for the full timeline with MRT routes, costs and maps.

Day 01 · Tuesday, 18 August

Landing Softly: A Ximending Day & Raohe Lights

A smart 2 AM arrival plan, a slow brunch at Fuhang Dou Jiang, then a whole afternoon in Ximending — murals, vintage shops, Donki, arcades, old-school coffee — before one direct train to Raohe Night Market and a riverside night walk.

🛬 Arrival day🎪 Ximending deep-dive🏮 Raohe Night Market🌉 Rainbow Bridge

Day 02 · Wednesday, 19 August

The Slow Classics: Huashan, 101 & a Dadaocheng Sunset

Sleep in until 8:30, wander Huashan's creative village in its quiet hour, splurge on Din Tai Fung and the 101 Observatory — then golden hour on 150-year-old Dihua Street and sunset over the river at Dadaocheng Wharf.

😴 8:30 wake-up🎨 Huashan 1914🏙️ 101 Observatory🌇 Dadaocheng sunset

Day 03 · Thursday, 20 August

Spirited Away: Shifen Lanterns & Jiufen Tea Houses

The unmissable day trip — release a sky lantern on Shifen's live railway tracks, walk to Taiwan's broadest waterfall, then graze Jiufen Old Street and sip gongfu tea at A-Mei Tea House as the red lanterns switch on.

🚂 Pingxi Line🏮 Sky lantern💦 Shifen Waterfall🍵 A-Mei Tea House

Day 04 · Friday, 21 August

One Last Breakfast, Then Home

A 10:20 AM international departure means a disciplined morning — wake at 6, Airport MRT Express at 7, checked in by 8. Plus a fallback plan if you oversleep, and where to grab last-minute pineapple cakes.

🛫 TPE 10:20 AM🚄 Airport MRT Express🥐 Breakfast plan
Open Day 4
🧭
The routing logic: Day 1 never leaves your Ximending neighborhood until one direct train to Raohe. Day 2 arcs east to the 101 and back west along the river for sunset. Day 3 leaves the city entirely — Shifen first because the waterfall closes ~5 PM, Jiufen second because its lanterns peak after 5:30 PM. Day 4 is pure logistics. No route is ever walked twice, and no morning starts before 8:30 unless a mountain town or a flight demands it.
🌦️
August weather rhythm: gentle outdoor blocks in the morning and after 4 PM, air-conditioned culture (and a scheduled coffee break) through the 2–4 PM thunderstorm window, evenings outdoors after the heat breaks. Every day below follows this pattern — see the Essentials page for the full weather brief.
