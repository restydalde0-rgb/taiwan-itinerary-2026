About This Planner

TAIWANDER ’26 is a personal travel planner for one very specific trip: two first-timers from Manila, four days and three nights in Taipei, 18–21 August 2026.

🧭 The Design Rules

  • 1️⃣Zero backtracking. Each day owns one part of the map; no route is walked twice.
  • 2️⃣Respect the August sun. Outdoor sights before noon, air-conditioning through the 2–4 PM thunderstorm window, night markets after dark.
  • 3️⃣Protect the arrival. The 2 AM landing gets a full plan of its own — sleep is itinerary item #1.
  • 4️⃣Food is the itinerary. Every meal slot has a named spot, a price, and a no-queue backup.
  • 5️⃣MRT-first. Taxis are fallbacks, not plans — every leg lists its transit route and fare.

🗺️ Why These Places

  • 🏮Raohe & Ningxia over bigger markets: the most photogenic and the most local-loved, respectively — quality over sprawl.
  • ⛰️Elephant Mountain because the best view of Taipei 101 is free and 25 minutes up.
  • 🚂Shifen before Jiufen because the waterfall closes ~5 PM and Jiufen's lanterns peak after 5:30 — the order is the trick.
  • 🏙️Ximending base because the Airport MRT, three metro lines and two food streets converge there.

⚙️ How It's Built

  • 🧱Plain HTML, CSS and JavaScript — no frameworks, no build step, GitHub Pages-ready.
  • 📱Mobile-first: designed to be used on a phone, on the MRT, with one thumb.
  • 🌙Dark mode for night-market navigation — toggle it in the top bar.
  • 💾The packing checklist saves to your device; no accounts, no tracking.
⚠️
Honest disclaimer: prices, opening hours, timetables and listings across this site are best-effort estimates as of July 2026. Restaurants close, Airbnbs rotate, typhoons happen. Re-verify anything time-critical — especially the Day 4 airport clock and Taiwan's entry rules for Philippine passport holders — in the week before flying.
